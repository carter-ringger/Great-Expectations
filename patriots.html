Carter Ringger's Spotlight:

Embracing the New England's Dynastic Mythos

By Carter Ringger | Updated on 15 April, 2023

Football is a multi-faceted game, in which a great deal of strategy and gameplanning, in addition to practice, must be involved to achieve proper on-field execution. All my life, the New England Patriots have been a symbol of dominance in the National Football League. Only in years following the separation of coach Bill Belichick and quarterback Tom Brady have I actually begun to appreciate their two decades of supremacy. While the players take the stage at the end of the day, I have found myself incredibly inspired by the dedication great coaches require of themselves. Stories began to come out after the Brady-Belichick split. I was amazed my the remarkable level of commitment demonstrated by these two men, specifically Bill Belichick.

Bill was both the last guy out of the building at night and the guy in his office at 4am the following morning. Bill developed compreghensive files, not just of the opposing players, but of the opposing coaches. He sought to master the game within the game by gathering information on their schemes, coaching philosophies, and tendencies in game-time situations. Players and coashing assistants alike have often lauded Bill for his attention to detail. Many corroborated stories recall Bill predicting how a game would unfold the following day, quarter by quarter. He'd follow his breakdown with further details on a gameplan that would minimize opponent strengths while maximizing theirs. Not only was Bill coaching the dynasty of the 2000s and 2010s during the regular season, but he also served as the team's general manager, scouting the collegiate landscape for potential roster additions.

Brady left the Patriots a year before I left for college and the Patriot influence became more apparent throughout my senior year, as I applied many of Belichickian principles to overcome personal hurdles. This interest grew as I moved off to college. I entered a competitive environment in BYU, an environment that could be very lonely. Jeff Benedict's The Dynasty and Seth Wickersham's It's Better to be Feared were scriptures to a young boy trying to develop an identity away from home. Entering my sophmore year, wearing a Patriots sweatshirt with cut-off sleeves has come to mean far more than simply showing team support; a mere change in attire was a reminder of my personal and conceptual conversion to the principles of Patriot way.

What does the Patriot Way represent?

I've never been able to adequately explain what the New England Patriots, or the Patriot Way, means to me personally. Below, I tried to summarize this deeply-heled personal belief into three words:

  1. Strategy
    • Applying hard work
    • Focusing on the details; Apply a fine-tooth comb
    • Finding ways to improve; Learning, regardless of outcome
    • Maintaining situational awareness
    • Accepting Accountability
    • Studying opponents schematic and personal tendencies
      • Searching for a competitive advantage
  2. Symbol
    • Investment in a mythic struggle
    • Searching for joy through achievement
      • Finding passion through a love of the game
    • Striving for excellence, mastery
    • Increasing intelligence
    • Continuing to fight, despite the odds
    • Not caring about the outside noise
    • Developing one’s cerebral nature
  3. Slogans
    • “You can’t win until you can keep from losing”
    • “Do Your Job”
    • “The Man in the Arena”
    • “Rough Rider”
